Lech Wałęsa

Fighting for Polish Freedom

Lech Wałęsa, standing on the left, addressing workers during the 1980 Gdansk Shipyard strike of Solidarność's success in reaching a deal with the government.

Wałęsa's History

  • 1943 - Born on 29th September at Popowo, near Wroclawek, Poland.
  • 1959 - Went to school in Lipno for two years. Enlisted in mandatory military service
  • 1967 - Became an electrician at the Lenin Shipyard in Gdansk.
  • 1976 - Lost his job after participating in protests, spent next 4 years of his life as an activist while under surveillance of Polish secret police.
  • 1980 - Following the election of John Paul II to the Papacy, workers across Poland undertook nationwide strikes. Wałęsa lead the strikes in the Lenin Shipyard, and eventually became the leader of the protest movement which was renamed to Solidarność, or Solidarity.
  • 1981 - Soviet pressure forced the Polish government to suppress protests and riots, resulting in martil law being declared on December 13 and the arrest of Wałęsa and other Solidarity leaders.
  • 1983 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1988 - New wave of protests resulted in Polish communists starting negotiations with Solidarity and Wałęsa. Solidarity became legal.
  • 1989 - Solidarity wins all available parliamentary positions in elections
  • 1990-1995 - Following the June 1989 legislative elections, Wałęsa becomes the first democratically elected President of Poland.
  • 1995 - Lost presidental elections, with less than 1% of the electorate's votes. Withdrew from politics.
  • 2000 - Lost another presidential election.
  • 2006 - Quit Solidarity in protest of Poland's right-wing Law and Justice party
  • 2011 - Receives Ronald Reagan Freedom Award
  • 2014 - Wałęsa criticizes Barack Obama's reception of the Nobel Peace Prize
